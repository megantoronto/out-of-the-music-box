Adjust Song Features

Type in a song and move the slider to adjust the features of your recommendations compared to the song you chose. For example, if you want your recommendations to be more danceable than your song, adjust the slider to “Slightly Higher”. When you’re done, hit submit and enjoy!

Danceability

Lower indicates recommendations should be less suitable for dancing than your song, higher indicates recommendations should be more danceable.

Energy

Lower indicates recommendations should be less intense (think Bach) than your song, higher indicates more intense (think death metal).

Loudness

Lower indicates recommendations should be less loud than your song, while higher indicates louder.

Speech

Lower indicates recommendations should be more musical than your song, higher indicates recommendations should have more spoken words (like poetry).

Acousticness

Lower indicates recommendations should be less acoustic than your song, higher indicates more acoustic recommendations.

Instrumentalness

Lower indicates recommendations should have more vocals than your song, higher indicates less vocal recommendations.

Liveness

Lower indicates recommendations should be studio recorded, while higher indicates live music.

Musical Valence

Lower indicates recommendations should sound more sad/angry than your song, higher indicates happier recommendations.

Lyrical Valence

Lower indicates recommendations should have more sad/angry lyrics than your song, higher indicates happier lyrics.

Tempo

Lower indicates recommendations should have a slower beat than your song, higher indicates a faster beat.

Key

Lower indicates the key of recommendations should be lower than your song, while higher indicates a higher key.

Why aren’t my recommendations next to the song I picked?

Each data point is one of the 250 most similar songs to your song based on your inputs. We’re sure you might be wondering why your song recommendations aren’t graphed right next to the song you picked. Music Box takes into account 11 different features, but unfortunately we can’t yet build a graph in 11-dimensional space. The graph you see reduces the 11 features down to 2-dimensional space; while the songs may look farther apart, they are the right recommendations!
